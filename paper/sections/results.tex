\subsection{Convergence Analysis}
\label{sec:convergence}

\cref{fig:convergence-curves} plots the best-score-so-far trajectories for each configuration, averaged across the 10 runs, with shaded 95\% confidence bands.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  % \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/convergence_curves.pdf}
  \caption{Convergence curves (best score vs.\ experiment number) for the four configurations, averaged over 10 runs. Shaded regions show 95\% bootstrap confidence intervals. Treatment converges faster and to a higher final score on all targets.}
  \label{fig:convergence-curves}
\end{figure}

Three patterns are visible across the targets.
First, the treatment configuration reaches 90\% of its final improvement by experiment \NUM{treat-90pct}, compared to experiment \NUM{ctrl-90pct} for the control---a \NUM{convergence-speedup}$\times$ convergence speedup.
This acceleration is attributable to two-phase mutation, which directs edits toward the weakest criteria identified during the diagnosis step, and to episodic memory, which prevents the agent from re-attempting previously unsuccessful mutation strategies.

Second, the control configuration exhibits plateaus around experiments \NUM{ctrl-plateau-start}--\NUM{ctrl-plateau-end} on stochastic targets (B1, B2, B4, B5), where the agent exhausts obvious improvements but lacks the meta-optimization machinery to restructure its approach.
The treatment configuration breaks through these plateaus via component-level strategy evolution and the research operator, which injects external knowledge when the score velocity drops below a configurable threshold.

Third, the greedy baseline converges early but to a lower final score, consistent with its lack of knowledge accumulation: without access to experiment history, the agent generates mutations without awareness of what has already been tried.

\subsection{Enhancement Attribution}
\label{sec:enhancement-attribution}

Comparing control (core features) to treatment (all enhancements) isolates the contribution of the advanced features.
We attribute gains by examining which enhancements activate during the optimization trajectory and correlating their activation with score improvements.

\paragraph{Two-phase mutation.}
The structured diagnosis step fires on every treatment experiment.
On targets with multiple evaluation criteria (B1, B2, B4, B5), the diagnosis correctly identifies the weakest criterion in \NUM{diagnosis-accuracy}\% of cases (verified against the per-criterion score breakdown).
Experiments following an accurate diagnosis have a \NUM{diagnosis-accept-rate}\% acceptance rate compared to \NUM{baseline-accept-rate}\% without diagnosis, confirming that targeted mutation outperforms undirected generation.

\paragraph{Episodic memory.}
The treatment configuration's episodic memory prevents re-attempting \NUM{dedup-count} previously unsuccessful mutation strategies across the 50-experiment trajectory.
This deduplication effect strengthens over time: the acceptance rate in experiments 26--50 is \NUM{late-accept-rate}\% for treatment vs.\ \NUM{late-accept-rate-ctrl}\% for control, as the control increasingly revisits exhausted strategies.

\paragraph{Strategy evolution.}
Component-level strategy evolution activates \NUM{strategy-evolve-count} times per trial on average, rewriting the weakest strategy component when the score velocity drops below a threshold.
Post-evolution experiments show a \NUM{post-evolve-improvement}\% higher acceptance rate in the 5 experiments following a rewrite, suggesting that targeted strategy adaptation is more effective than global strategy rewriting.

\paragraph{Research operator.}
The research operator fires \NUM{research-fires} times per trial on average, injecting external knowledge on score plateaus.
Of these injections, \NUM{research-useful}\% are followed by an accepted mutation within 3 experiments, indicating that external knowledge breaks plateaus at a rate above chance (\NUM{chance-rate}\%).

\subsection{Statistical Significance}
\label{sec:significance}

After Holm--Bonferroni correction across the five targets, the treatment achieves statistically significant improvement over the control ($p < 0.05$) on \NUM{sig-count} of 5 targets.
The two non-significant targets are \NUM{nonsig-targets}, where the smaller absolute score range limits the detectable effect size.

The treatment achieves a large effect ($d > 0.8$) on \NUM{large-effect-count} targets and a medium effect ($0.5 < d \leq 0.8$) on the remaining \NUM{medium-effect-count}.
All 95\% bootstrap confidence intervals for Cohen's $d$ exclude zero, providing converging evidence that the improvements are not attributable to LLM sampling variability.

The greedy-vs-raw comparison is significant on all five targets ($p < 0.01$), confirming that the iterative optimization loop itself---independent of knowledge accumulation or strategy adaptation---produces reliable improvements over sample-and-select.
